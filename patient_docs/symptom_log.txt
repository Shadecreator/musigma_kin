==============================
JANUARY 2026
==============================

Jan 10 (Sat)
GP appointment today. I took the morning off work. Dad didn't want me to come
but I insisted. Dr. Nair was lovely. She noticed his HbA1c has gone up again.
He's been skipping his evening meds, I've seen the pills still on the counter
some mornings. Told her about it. Dad got a bit embarrassed. She was kind about it.
BP was 138/86 which she said was okay-ish. He seemed sharp today, made a joke
about the weighing scale being wrong.

Jan 13 (Tue)
Called in the evening. He sounded tired. Said he'd had a "quiet day." When I
asked if he'd eaten properly he said "yes yes don't fuss." Noticed Metoprolol
still in the blister pack when I came by Sunday -- he'd missed at least 2 this week.

Jan 19 (Mon) -- BAD NIGHT
He called me at 6:15am. Said he'd had a "strange night." When I asked what he
meant he said he kept waking up, maybe 4 or 5 times, and at one point he couldn't
remember where he was for a minute. He laughed it off. Said he must have been
dreaming. I don't think he was just dreaming. He sounded shaken.
His voice was normal by the time I got there at 8am but he looked exhausted.
Dark circles. Didn't finish his breakfast.

Jan 20 (Tue)
Went over after work. He seemed back to normal but quieter than usual. Didn't
want to watch his evening TV show, went to bed at 8:30pm. That's very early for him.
He said "I just feel foggy." That word again. Foggy.

Jan 27 (Tue)
Good week overall. He walked to the market on Sunday which he loves. Called to tell
me he bought mangoes. This is the dad I know.

==============================
FEBRUARY 2026
==============================

Feb 3 (Tue)
Cardiology appointment. Dr. Iyer was thorough. He did an echo and ECG.
Said heart function looks okay -- "preserved ejection fraction" -- but there's
some thickening from years of high blood pressure. He measured his blood pressure
standing up and sitting down and said there's a significant drop when he stands.
That explains the dizziness. He said rise slowly, drink more water.
When I mentioned the confusion episodes he said it wasn't likely from his heart
but I should follow up with the GP for a neurology referral if it keeps happening.
Dad came out of that appointment looking reassured. I came out with more questions.

Feb 8 (Sun) -- BAD NIGHT
I was at his place for Sunday dinner. He seemed a bit quieter than usual at dinner
but I thought he was just tired. I left around 9pm.
He called me at 2:30am. He was confused. Kept asking me "where is Meena?" -- that's
my mum, she passed in 2021. I talked to him for about 15 minutes. He slowly came
back. By the time I got there (30 mins) he knew who I was, knew where he was.
He was embarrassed and kept saying "I must have been half asleep."
I sat with him until 5am then went home and cried in the car.
I haven't told my husband how scared I am.

Feb 9 (Mon)
He remembered the call but not the confusion. Said he was "fine now."
I noticed his evening meds from the previous 3 nights were all still in the pack.
Three nights of missed Metoprolol and Potassium Chloride.
I set up an alarm on his phone for 8pm labelled "EVENING MEDS." He said he didn't
need it. I set it up anyway.

Feb 14 (Sat)
Valentine's Day. He called to tell me he'd seen a film on TV that he and Mum used
to watch together. He sounded sad but clear-headed. Normal conversation.
I feel relieved on good days and then guilty for feeling relieved.

Feb 22 (Sun)
Came for Sunday lunch. He seemed good. His evening meds alarm has been going off --
I checked the pill organiser and it looks like he's been more consistent this week.
Maybe 1 missed in the whole week instead of 3-4. Small win.

==============================
MARCH 2026
==============================

Mar 2 (Mon)
He mentioned his legs felt "heavy" in the morning. He said it's been happening
a few days. Could be the Furosemide not doing enough? Or maybe he's not drinking
enough water? I texted Dr. Nair's clinic to ask. They said to monitor and come in
if it worsens or if he has breathlessness.

Mar 8 (Sun)
Sunday visit. He looked tired. Said he'd been waking up a lot at night.
"Too many thoughts," he said. I asked what thoughts. He said "nothing, just thoughts."
I think he's anxious but won't say it directly. He's from a generation that doesn't
talk about that.

Mar 14 (Sat) -- BAD NIGHT
I was there for the weekend. He went to bed at 10pm.
At around 1:30am I heard him up. I found him in the kitchen standing at the counter
looking confused. He had a glass in his hand but didn't seem to know why.
I said "Dad, come back to bed" and he looked at me like he was trying to place me.
It lasted maybe 10 minutes. Then he was fine. He said "I was just getting water."
I don't think he knew what he was doing.
Sat with him until he fell asleep again. I didn't sleep after that.

Mar 15 (Sun)
He woke up and had no memory of the kitchen episode. I told him gently what
happened. He got quiet. Said "it's nothing, I'm just getting old."
I told him I was going to call the doctor on Monday.
He said "don't make a fuss."
I called the doctor anyway.

Mar 17 (Tue)
GP clinic call. Nurse advised to come in if it happens again and to go to the ER
immediately if he seems confused for more than 30 minutes or can't recognise me.
GP appointment scheduled for Apr 15.

Mar 22 (Sun) -- ER VISIT
I was at home when he called at 2am. He was incoherent. I couldn't understand what
he was saying. I drove over. He was standing in the hallway, didn't recognise me
for about 5 minutes. That's never happened before. I drove him to City General.
We were there until nearly 8am. CT scan was clear. Blood sugar was fine.
They said it was probably sleep deprivation and possible medication issues.
His potassium was 3.4 -- the doctor said that was low-normal and flagged it.
I asked if the missed Potassium Chloride could cause this. She said "possibly
contributing."
We came home. He slept all day. I sat on the sofa and didn't know what to do with myself.

Mar 25 (Wed)
He's quieter since the ER visit. Less likely to walk outside. He keeps saying
"I don't want to go too far from home." I think the episode frightened him.
He hasn't been to the market since. He used to go every Sunday.

Mar 29 (Sun)
He didn't want to do much today. Watched TV mostly. His steps have really dropped --
I can see it on the Fitbit app. He used to average 5000+ steps. This past week he's
barely hitting 2000 some days.
Evening meds compliance better this week -- I've been texting him at 8pm every night
as a reminder and he's been responding with the thumbs up emoji.

==============================
APRIL 2026
==============================

Apr 4 (Sat) -- BAD NIGHT
I was worried when he didn't answer my 8pm text. Called -- he answered, said he'd
already gone to bed. It was 7:45pm. Very early.
He called me at 3am. He was confused again. This time shorter -- about 8 minutes.
But this is the 5th time now.
I wrote it down immediately this time: 3:04am, called Sara, confused, asked for
"the key to the school" (he was a teacher for 35 years), back to normal by 3:12am.
I am making a list of every episode now. There is a pattern.
Every single one has happened after a bad night's sleep.

Apr 5 (Sun)
He has no memory of the call. He made chai and offered me biscuits like nothing
happened.
I showed him the list. He read it quietly. He said "okay beti, you should bring
this to the doctor." That's the first time he's said that.
I'm bringing everything to cardiology on the 30th.

Apr 10 (Fri)
Lab results back from Dr. Nair's requested panel. HbA1c is now 7.6%, up from 7.4%.
She wants to review diabetes management. I've booked that for May.
Focusing on the cardiology appointment first.

He called tonight just to chat. We talked about a cricket match for 20 minutes.
He was sharp, funny, himself.
I need to hold onto these moments.

-- Sara
